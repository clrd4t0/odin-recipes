Japanese Cheesecake

Japanese Cheesecake. Image Source

This recipe, courtesy of Christine's Recipes, will guide you to make a beautiful fluffy Japanese Cheesecake that will keep everyone asking for more!

Ingredients

To follow along, you will need the following ingredients:

  • Milk - 250ml
  • Cream Cheese (Diced and Softened) - 250g
  • Butter (Softened) - 60g
  • Egg Yolk - 6
  • Cake Flour - 55g
  • Corn Starch - 20g
  • Lemon Zests (Yellow Parts Only) - 1 (whole lemnon)
  • Egg White - 6
  • Cream of Tartar - 1/4 Teaspoon
    Can be replaced wth 2 teaspoons of lemon juice
  • Caster Sugar - 130g
You will also need baking trays with a dimension of 11.5×22×6cm.

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 150C. Put baking paper into the baking trays.
  2. Pour the milk into a big bowl, and warm it up over a pot of water with small heat. The bowl should not boe in contact with the hot water. Add the cream cheese and keep stirring until cheese is completely melted with a smooth consistency and no solids. Add the butter and keep stirring until butter is completely melted. Remove the bowl from the pot of hot water and let it rest on the side.
  3. Mix the corn starch and cake flour, sieve the results into the cheese mixture in three batches. Make sure the mixture is completely mixed with no lumps before adding the next batch. Mix in the lemon zests. Put the resulting batter aside for later.
  4. Put the egg whites into a bowl. Make sure the bowl is free of oil and water.Use an eletric mixer to beat the egg whites until bubbles appear. Add the cream of tartar and keep mixing. Add the caster sugar in three patches. Keep beating until egg white is stiff.
  5. Use a spatula to add the egg whites into the batter in three batches.
  6. Pour the results into the two baking trays and put both trays into a bigger tray. Fill the bigger tray with water until half full. Place the tray into the lower rack of the oven and bake for 50 to 60 minutes. Insert a needle or toothpick into the middle of the cake and take it out. If no batter is stuck onto the needle when it is removed, the cake is done. Turn off the heat and open the door for a couple inches. Wait 10 minutes before removing the cake from the oven. Remove the cake from the tray and place them of the rack for cooling. After they're cooled down, place them into the fridge for 3 hours
